## Purpose

This article provides basic troubleshooting steps for resolving issues when a desktop or laptop computer will not power on.

## Common Symptoms

- Computer does not turn on
- No power light
- No fan noise
- No display on screen
- Laptop does not charge
- Device turns on and immediately shuts down
- Power button does not respond
- Docking station does not power the laptop
- Monitor shows no signal
- Device makes beep sounds or shows blinking LED lights

## Quick Checks

1. Confirm whether the device is a laptop or desktop.
2. Ask the user what happens when they press the power button.
3. Check if there are any lights, sounds, fan movement, or error messages.
4. Confirm the power cable is connected securely.
5. Test a different power outlet if available.
6. For laptops, check the charger and charging light.
7. For desktops, check the power cable, monitor cable, and power switch.
8. Remove unnecessary external devices such as USB drives, docking stations, and peripherals.
9. Do not open the device or remove internal parts unless authorized.

## Troubleshooting Steps

### 1. Confirm the Power Issue

Ask the user:

- Does the computer show any lights?
- Do you hear the fan or any startup sound?
- Does the screen turn on?
- Does the device turn on and then shut off?
- Did the issue start after travel, a power outage, liquid spill, drop, or update?
- Is the device connected to a docking station?
- Is this affecting one device or multiple devices?

If multiple devices are affected, check for a power outage, power strip issue, or office electrical issue.

### 2. Check the Power Source

Verify the computer is connected to a working power source.

Steps:

1. Confirm the power cable is fully connected.
2. Try a different wall outlet.
3. Avoid using a power strip for testing if possible.
4. If using a surge protector, confirm it is switched on.
5. Test another device in the same outlet if available.

If the outlet does not work, move the device to another outlet and report the electrical issue if needed.

### 3. Check the Power Cable or Adapter

For desktops:

- Confirm the power cable is securely connected to the back of the computer.
- Confirm the power cable is connected to the wall outlet.
- Check if the power supply switch on the back of the desktop is turned on, if available.
- Try a known working power cable if available.

For laptops:

- Confirm the charger is connected securely to the laptop and wall outlet.
- Check for a charging light on the laptop.
- Check the power adapter light if it has one.
- Try a known working charger if available and compatible.
- Inspect the charger for visible damage.

Do not use an incompatible charger.

### 4. Check the Battery or Charging Status

For laptops, confirm whether the battery may be fully drained.

Steps:

1. Connect the laptop to power.
2. Wait 5 to 10 minutes.
3. Check for a charging indicator.
4. Press the power button again.

If the battery is removable and company policy allows it, remove and reconnect the battery.  
If the battery is not removable, do not open the device.

### 5. Perform a Hard Power Reset

A hard power reset can clear temporary power issues.

For laptops:

1. Disconnect the charger.
2. Remove external devices such as USB drives, external monitors, and docking stations.
3. Hold the power button for 15 to 30 seconds.
4. Reconnect the charger.
5. Press the power button again.

For desktops:

1. Disconnect the power cable.
2. Hold the power button for 15 to 30 seconds.
3. Reconnect the power cable.
4. Press the power button again.

Follow company policy before performing hardware resets.

### 6. Remove External Devices

Disconnect unnecessary external devices and test power again.

Examples:

- USB drives
- External hard drives
- Docking station
- External monitor
- Keyboard and mouse
- Printers
- Headsets
- Card readers

A faulty external device can sometimes prevent normal startup.

### 7. Check the Display

Sometimes the computer is powered on, but the screen is blank.

Check:

- Power light on the computer
- Fan noise
- Keyboard lights
- Monitor power light
- Monitor cable connection
- Monitor input/source
- Brightness level on laptop
- External monitor connection

For desktops, confirm the monitor is powered on and connected to the correct port.

If the computer appears to be on but there is no display, troubleshoot the monitor or display connection separately.

### 8. Check Docking Station Issues

If the laptop is connected to a docking station, remove it from the dock and test directly.

Steps:

1. Disconnect the laptop from the docking station.
2. Connect the charger directly to the laptop.
3. Press the power button.
4. If the laptop powers on, reconnect the dock and test again.

If the issue only happens when using the dock, troubleshoot the docking station, power adapter, or dock firmware.

### 9. Check for Beep Codes or LED Patterns

If the computer shows blinking lights or makes beep sounds, document the pattern.

Examples:

- Number of beeps
- Color of LED light
- Blinking pattern
- Error message on screen
- Manufacturer logo or diagnostic screen

Do not guess the meaning of the code. Document it and escalate or check the manufacturer support guide.

### 10. Test with Known Working Equipment

If available and allowed by company policy, test with known working equipment.

Examples:

- Power cable
- Laptop charger
- Wall outlet
- Monitor cable
- Docking station
- External monitor

This helps identify whether the issue is with the computer, power adapter, cable, dock, or display.

## Escalation Criteria

Escalate the issue to Tier 2, desktop support, hardware support, or vendor support if:

- The device still does not power on after basic troubleshooting
- There are no lights, no fan noise, and no response from the power button
- The charger or power adapter appears damaged
- The device was dropped or exposed to liquid
- The battery may have failed
- The power port appears loose or damaged
- The device powers on and immediately shuts down repeatedly
- Beep codes or LED diagnostic patterns appear
- The issue may involve motherboard, power supply, battery, or internal hardware failure
- The user cannot complete critical work and needs a loaner device

## Documentation Example

User reported that their laptop would not power on. Confirmed there were no lights or fan noise when pressing the power button. Checked the wall outlet and confirmed it was working. Verified the charger was connected securely, but no charging light appeared. Removed the laptop from the docking station and connected the charger directly to the laptop. Performed a hard power reset by holding the power button for 30 seconds, then reconnected power. Laptop powered on successfully and reached the Windows login screen. User confirmed the device stayed powered on.

## Resolution

Issue resolved after disconnecting the docking station and performing a hard power reset.

## Verification

Confirmed the laptop powered on successfully, reached the Windows login screen, and stayed powered on while connected to the charger. User confirmed they were able to log in and continue working.

## Prevention / Notes

- Confirm power cables and chargers are connected securely.
- Avoid using damaged chargers, cables, or power adapters.
- Use approved chargers and docking stations only.
- Report repeated power issues for hardware review.
- Document all visible indicators, such as LED lights, beep codes, fan noise, and screen behavior.
- Escalate suspected hardware failures instead of opening the device without authorization.
